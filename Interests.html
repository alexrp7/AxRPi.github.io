Being Me

Eritrea is a young East-African country bordering much of the Red Sea. My parents left as teenage refugees and eventually met in the United States while completing their college educations.

Electrical Engineering wasn't always my first choice. I originally wanted to go into physics and computer science. However, I could not and still cannot think of a btter merging of the two. There is, and will always be, time to learn more programming languages. Practicing coding styles or language syntax can be done with a laptop. However, circuits and solid state components require a more hands-on approach to really understand what is going on. I chose to spend my time with what I knew would not be readily available down the line. Now I find myself working towards my PhD. Now at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, I am hopefully moving towards a dregree in photonics. I started inside a image sensor group working on an ultraviolet-color image sensor/system. Towards the end of my first semester I switched to the creation and characterization of an address event representation image sensor with a polarization filter on top. Why Interstellar and Arrival? Well, the former is probably my favorite movie of all time. While its story may be flawed, the dedication to science and the representation of scientits & engineers as the vital characters left me in awe. Interstellar has gorgeous visual effects that use real math and physics to depict more realistic representations of space-time anomalies. Additionally, the soundtrack was composed by Hans Zimmer — which is a work of art in its own regard. On the other hand, Arrival has a story worth listnening to and a plot with incredible meaning.
